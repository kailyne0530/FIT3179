Population Density

Which countries are most densely populated?

Numerous small islands around the world has significant population numbers relative to their land size. Over the past half-decade, the three countries exhibiting the highest population density have been Macau, Monaco, and Singapore. For instance, Macau supports a staggering 23,000 people per square mile, making it more than 600 times denser than the United States and nearly 8,000 times denser than Australia.

Among the larger countries, Bangladesh takes the lead in terms of population density, accommodating over 1,000 people per square mile. This figure is nearly three times greater than that of its neighbor, India. On the other hand, Australia, with an approximate population density of 3.4 people per square mile, stands as one of the least densely populated nations globally, alongside Greenland, Mongolia, and Namibia.

Drivers of Population Growth

What have been the forces behind the dramatic increase in population?

Birth Rate vs Death Rate

Over the past five years, an intriguing pattern emerges when birth and death rates are examined across continents. Asia consistently maintains relatively lower birth and death rates compared to other continents. In stark contrast, Africa stands out with significantly higher birth and death rates. Europe, on the other hand, registers relatively elevated death rates compared to its global counterparts. Oceania, South America and North America tend to exhibit moderate birth and death rates.

While some countries have witnessed a noteworthy increase in birth rates in the last five years, these increments pale in comparison to the substantial declines observed in other nations. For instance, Hong Kong has experienced a notable 37% decrease in its birth rate, while China follows closely with a 31% decline. These significant reductions in birth rates have transformative implications for demographic structures and social dynamics.

Globally, the minimum decrease in death rates hovers just below 8%. However, certain countries have encountered a stark and unsettling increase in death rates. Ukraine, for instance, has seen a dramatic 63% spike in its death rate, while Lebanon faces an alarming 88% surge. These sudden increases in death rates are likely attributed to the complex interplay of factors, including political instability and conflicts, casting a somber shadow on the population's well-being.

Migration

While international migration is a relatively small contributor to overall population change when compared to births or deaths, its significance cannot be overlooked. The number of international migrants has increased acrosss all continents. Between 1990 and 2020, Europe and Asia stood out as major hosts for international migrants, with each continent hosting approximately 87 and 86 million migrants, respectively. These numbers accounted for a significant 61% of the global international migrant population.

Notably, Asia witnessed a remarkable surge in international migration from 2000 to 2020, experiencing a staggering 74% growth. Europe, following closely behind, recorded the second-largest increase during this period, with an additional 30 million international migrants. North America and Africa also saw considerable growth, with an increase of 18 million and 10 million international migrants, respectively. There can be significant variations in migrant populations within Oceania and South America due to specific economic, political or historical factors.

Increase in Longevity

Life expectancy has increased considerably in recent decades and this trend is set to continue. In 1950, life expectancy varied widely among nations, with countries like the United States and Australia leading, while nations such as China and India lagged with shorter average lifespans. However, by 2022, remarkable improvements were evident across the board, attributed to medical breakthroughs, economic growth, and global health initiatives. Japan, with its advanced healthcare, stood out with life expectancy in the late 80s. Overall, the upward trend in life expectancy from 1950 to 2022 has played a significant role in shaping global population growth patterns, emphasising the complex interplay between health, longevity, and demographics.

Ranking by Country

Population by Age Group

Recent years have seen a consistent pattern in the highest global rates of birth, death, and migration. Philippines continues to lead with the highest birth rate, whereas India, despite registering the lowest birth rate among them, paradoxically also holds the highest death rate. United States persistently exhibits the highest migration rate. This trend potentially attribute to its relatively higher living standard and comprehensive educational opportunities. Notably, these rankings have demonstrated remarkable stability over time, suggesting that the underlying drivers of population growth are deeply rooted and change gradually.

Simultaneously, there is a growing global trend of an aging population, significantly impacting countries differently. China's aging demographic, potentially a consequence of its former one-child policy, starkly contrasts with South Africa's increasing youth population. Meanwhile, Australia's demographic trends might be a ripple effect from the 1970s baby boom, and Japan's significant elderly population is likely due to its leading life expectancy rates. These patterns underscore the diverse demographic paths and challenges various nations face, driven by unique historical, cultural, and socio-economic narratives.
